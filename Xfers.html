xfers Mobile App Conceptualization

Announced at the beginning of 2016, Singapore peer-to-peer payments platform xfers raised US$2.5 million in seed funding eyeing expansion in Indonesia. xfers is a payment gateway that makes it easy for anyone with an online bank account to collect payments and make payouts automatically. After being addicted to Venmo for a year, I became a huge fan of P2P Payments. xfers currently runs on web, but not on mobile yet. This gave me an idea to conceptualize a mobile app.

Disclaimer: I don't have a working relationship with xfers, this is a self-initiated project.

Analyzing the Web Platform

As I entered xfers' dashboard, I took a quick glance at the information presented and thought it was straightforward and easy to understand. As the amount of information on the dashboard is not overwhelming, it is not difficult to turn this into a mobile interface.

Designing a Mobile Dashboard

Since I don't intend to design a mobile app for the entire platform, I defined the project scope to designing a mobile dashboard. I began by mapping out the top user concerns when viewing the dashboard.

  • How much money do I have?
  • What are my latest transactions?
  • How have I been managing my money overtime?
  • Which bank account am I using
  • Is the bank operating it services now?

Mapping a User Journey

With the 5 user needs in mind, I mapped out the user journey according to the hierarchy of needs.

Sketching

As there are many ways to present the information in the dashboard, I did a quick sketch on the type of layouts that would make it easy for the users to glance through quickly.

Creating Wireframes

Narrowing down from the sketches, I designed the app by giving it a simple and clean layout with reference to Google Material Design guidelines.

Final Result

The colorful scheme of the user interface is influenced by the brand color. As this is a payments app, the tone of the interface has to make the users feel secure transacting money with this app.
