P2P Payments Concept


Ever since I started using Venmo when I was living in New York for a year, my life changed. Gone are the days you need to make a trip to withdraw cash from the ATM to pay a friend, or feel embarassed asking money back from someone.

The rise of Peer to Peer Payments apps and services in Singapore is opening up new possibilities for our payments scene and creating real shifts in our daily lives. DBS Paylah or OCBC PayAnyone are fragmented and only available for those with designated bank accounts. The best thing in the market now would the newly launched PayNow that connects all the 7 banks together.



Seizing the perfect timing, Grab joins in the game by launching Peer-to-Peer fund transfer in GrabPay Mobile Wallet in Singapore.



What if C2C Marketplaces, like Carousell, launched payments too?


In an open marketplace like Carousell, both buyers and sellers are vulnerable towards issues like No-shows or Frauds. What could be the catalyst for these problems?




From the user journey, we could see that the issues occur at the stage where sellers and buyers are left to handle their payments on their own. If this was a responsibility that Carousell would like to take on, there could be ways to prevent no-shows or fraud from happening.

A Design Concept for Carousell Wallet


Having a wallet could help resolve no-show cases as buyers have to pay upfront to reserve their order. As this definitely opens up risk for fraud cases, a wallet has the capability to withhold the payment from the seller until the buyer receives the item.

More Possibilities





Coming from my experience in Xfers, the capabilities of a wallet is far more powerful than anyone can imagine. It is up to companies to constantly explore how to use and evolve the wallet towards their advantage.

Market Validation and Trends


It is easy to suggest ideas and design concepts that we think might work. More importantly, we need to look around at what the "big boys" have done, and the opportunities available.


Example 1: Alipay aims to become an All-In-One payment platform

“The new Alipay app is no longer a wallet,” said Fan Zhiming, president of Ant Financial’s payment business unit. “It is a platform which will change the way you shop, the way you spend and the way you manage your finances. It is not just another app, it is the super app tailored for every individual consumer.” (Payment Week, July 2015)

Example 2: Tokopedia offering multiple payment options

What if companies do not want to engage in a wallet play? Tokopedia serves as a great example of providing a full suite of payment options for all types of customers. This is also an effect of the low credit card penetration rate in Indonesia, and bank transfer / cash being the preferred payment methods there.
