Before I Met You

Conceived as an experiment in profiling and text analysis, Before I Met You is a participatory digital art piece driven by the notion of true empathy. If you have ever felt that someone does not understand how you feel, we would like you to examine the origin of the gap. Where does it come from? How can we explore ways to bridge this disconnection?

This project will be exhibited at ArtScience Museum as part of Betwixt Festival: Art & Bytes

Inspiration: Empathy Match Algorithm

Imagine that you’re programming in the middle of the night, you’re frustrated because the deadline is tomorrow and your teammates are not doing their part. You want to find someone who you can talk to, but not your friends because you think that they won’t understand. With Empathy Match, we will find you someone that CAN understand instantly. It helps you save time from explaining, makes you feel better, and you can dive straight back into work after a good chat.

Video Demo

This demo shows what happens when we try to match entries from 2 different users. For demo purposes, we took song lyrics from Adele's Hello and Lady Gaga's alejandro.

How did we build this?

Empathy Match is built based on JavaScript framework called Meteor.js and IBM Watson Personality Insights API to analyze the emotions and experience of the participants through semantic text analysis.

The Design Process

Mapping a User Flowchart

Before my team get to designing the interface, we thought out the flow of how we want our users to go through this experience.

Conducting User Tests

As the matching can get complicated, we needed to collect an initial pool of entries to test our algorithm. More importantly, we needed to test if people are keen to participate and comfortable to share their feelings. To begin, we used Google Forms to simulate our actual interface. We got 18 different entries for the test.

Creating Wireframes

Narrowing down from the sketches, I designed the app by giving it a simple and clean layout with reference to Google Material Design guidelines.

Planning for a Museum Experience

As this project involves more than just a software but also an art exhibition, we has to take into account of the surroundings as well.
